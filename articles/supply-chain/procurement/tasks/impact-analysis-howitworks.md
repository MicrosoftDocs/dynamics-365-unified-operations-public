#How impact analysis works#

When a purchase order response is received with a date or quantity change to a line (including cancellation), the Impact Analysis agent automatically identifies which downstream orders and inventory levels are affected. This helps purchasing teams understand the full scope of a supply chain disruption before taking action.
## Changes to Purchase Order Lines That Trigger an Impact Analysis

- Delivery Date (“Confirmed Delivery Date”)
- Quantity
- Cancellations (where quantity is changed to 0)

**Note:** The following changes are recognized when communicated via e-mail by Supplier Communications Agent, but do not trigger an impact analysis:
- Unit of measure
- Price
- Confirmation
- Quantity increases
- Earlier delivery dates


## How Impact is Calculated

The agent traces the changed purchase order through your supply chain to find connected downstream demand. It does this by:

1. **Tracing direct connections** - Finding orders that are explicitly linked to the purchase order through markings (reservations that directly tie supply to demand)

2. **Tracing planned connections** - Following the supply-demand chain through pegging relationships in your active dynamic plan to find which planned and confirmed orders depend on this supply

3. **Tracing project connections** - Checking if the purchase order is linked to a project.
The agent follows these connections recursively, meaning it can identify multi-level chains of impact. For example, if a purchase order supplies a production order, which in turn supplies a sales order, the agent will identify the entire chain.

### What Can Be Impacted

The impact analysis can identify effects on:

**Downstream Orders:**
- **Sales orders** - Customer orders that may be delayed or cannot be fulfilled
- **Production orders** - Manufacturing orders that depend on the supply
- **Transfer orders** - Warehouse transfers that rely on the item
- **Forecast orders** - Demand forecast requirements used by the planning system to anticipate and prepare for expected future demand
- **Safety stock requirements** - Planned requirements generated by the planning system to maintain minimum inventory levels. These appear when the planning system has created planned orders to replenish safety stock, and the purchase order change affects the ability to fulfill those planned requirements.


**Inventory Levels:**
- **Below safety stock minimum** - Inventory drops below configured safety stock (buffer consumed)
- **Negative inventory (stockout)** - Inventory goes below zero (cannot fulfill orders)

The agent traces through multi-level scenarios, such as:
- Purchase order → Production order(s) → Sales order(s)
- Purchase order → Transfer order(s) → Sales order(s)
> **Note**: The agent respects all relevant planning dimensions (site, warehouse, inventory status, product configuration) to ensure supply and demand are matched correctly.

## Classifying if a change has impact
Changes are classified as:
- **Has impact** - The change delays downstream orders and/or causes inventory levels to fall below minimum requirements
- **No impact** - The change does not delay downstream orders or cause inventory to drop below minimum levels

### “Has impact” scenarios
The agent flags impact when:
- **Downstream orders will be delayed** - The supply date moves after the delivery date required by connected demand
- **Quantity reductions affect coverage** - The reduced supply quantity is insufficient to cover downstream demand
- **Inventory threshold breaches** - The change causes inventory levels to drop below critical thresholds:
 	 - **Below minimum** - On-hand inventory falls below the configured safety stock level (but remains positive)
 	 - **Negative (stockout)** - On-hand inventory goes below zero, resulting in a complete stockout

### “No impact” scenarios
No impact is indicated when:
- **No downstream orders found** - The purchase order is not linked to any dependent demand
- **Sufficient buffer exists** - Inventory levels and timing provide adequate buffer to absorb the change
- **Change is an improvement** - The purchase order date moves earlier or quantity increases (positive changes don't flag impact)
